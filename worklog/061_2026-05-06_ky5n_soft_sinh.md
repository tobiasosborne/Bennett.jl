## Session log ‚Äî 2026-05-06 ‚Äî Bennett-ky5n / Tier C1.7 ‚Äî `soft_sinh` + `llvm.sinh` + libm `@sinh` dispatch (second hyperbolic)

**Why:** continuing the C1 grind requested by the user. Filed Bennett-

**Algorithm decision (3+1 synthesis).** Two parallel `Plan` proposers
explored the design space. Proposer A used a 4-regime split with
verbatim Julia stdlib thresholds (`SINH_SMALL_X = 2.1`, `H_LARGE_X
= 709.78`) and a Taylor polynomial. Proposer B argued for a
**unified exp formula** `(E¬≤ - 1/E¬≤)/2` with `E = exp(|x|/2)` to
collapse medium+huge into a single arm ‚Äî clever, but empirically
the 5 chained `soft_fmul` operations in that form accumulated
~2.5-3 ULP rounding, hitting **3-4 ULP at |x| ‚âà 1.4**. So:

- Started with Proposer B's strategy (one regime for non-poly).
- Smoke + random sweep showed 15-23 failures > 2 ULP per seed.
- Pinpointed the failures at small-magnitude medium inputs (1.0 < |x| < 2).
- **Switched to Proposer A's regime-selected exp-arg design**:
  `arg = ifelse(is_huge, |x|/2, |x|); E = soft_exp_fast(arg)`,
  medium uses `(E - 1/E)/2` (3 ops after exp; ~1.5 ULP rounding),
  huge uses `(0.5¬∑E)¬∑E` with load-bearing operator ordering.
- **ONE soft_exp_fast call total** via the regime-selected argument
  (the medium and huge formulas share E even though they want different
  arguments ‚Äî Bennett's eager-evaluation model means the "wrong" arm
  computes garbage that's discarded by the cascade).
- Re-ran sweep: max 2 ULP, 0 failures across 300k random samples √ó 3 seeds.

**Polynomial regime narrowed to `|x| ‚â§ 1.0`** (vs Julia stdlib's
`|x| ‚â§ 2.1`). Justification: Julia uses double-double `sinh_kernel`
(via `two_mul` + `exthorner`) to achieve 1 ULP at the 2.1 boundary.
Bennett.jl has no DD primitives; single-precision Horner accumulates
~9¬∑Œµ rounding at the boundary, which would breach the 2-ULP budget on
`|x| > ~1.5`. Narrowing to `|x| ‚â§ 1.0` keeps the polynomial within
budget by inspection. The medium-regime formula `(E - 1/E)/2` covers
`(1.0, 709.0]` with cancellation loss ~0.21 bits at the boundary ‚Äî
plenty of headroom.

**Drive-by finding: `soft_exp_fast` NaN bug at the realmax boundary.**
For inputs in `(~709.78, ~709.79)`, `soft_exp_fast` returns NaN
instead of a finite value just under realmax. (Outside this narrow
range, `soft_exp_fast` returns finite or +Inf correctly.) Worked
around in this bead by setting the medium‚Üîhuge threshold
conservatively at `709.0` (well below the NaN range), so the medium
arm's `soft_exp_fast(|x|)` call always lands in the verified-finite
range. **Filed as a separate concern** ‚Äî this should become its own
bead later (the bug affects soft_exp_fast/soft_exp users beyond just
sinh; tanh's `soft_exp_fast(2|x|)` argument max is 44.0 so it's
unaffected, but a future user could hit this).

**Gotchas / Lessons:**

1. **`soft_exp_fast` NaN range bug** at `(~709.78, ~709.79)` ‚Äî NOT
   in this bead's scope to fix. Tanh wasn't affected (its exp arg
   maxes at `2¬∑22 = 44`). Future bead should investigate
   fexp.jl's `_MAX_EXP_E_BITS` overflow predicate; the threshold
   appears slightly too low so inputs ~1 ULP below it produce NaN.
2. **`(E - 1/E)/2` beats `(E¬≤ - 1/E¬≤)/2` on rounding.** Both
   formulas are mathematically identical, but the squared form has
   5 chained soft_fmul ops vs the linear form's 3 ops after exp.
   Empirically: 5 ops √ó ~0.5 ULP each = ~2.5 ULP cumulative,
   amplified ~1.06√ó by cancellation at small |x| ‚Üí 3-4 ULP. The
   linear form is ~1.5 ULP cumulative + amplification ‚Üí ~2 ULP.
   General rule: when you have a choice of algebraically equivalent
   formulas, pick the one with fewer chained soft-float ops.
3. **Operator ordering in `(0.5¬∑E)¬∑E` is load-bearing**, not just
   for clarity. Naive `(E¬∑E)¬∑0.5` overflows at `|x| ‚âà 710` where
   true sinh is finite (up to `|x| ‚âà 710.476`). The correct order
   `(0.5¬∑E)¬∑E` halves the dominant term BEFORE squaring, delaying
   overflow until `|x| ‚âà 1419` exactly when true sinh transitions
   to ¬±Inf. **Inline comment in fsinh.jl flags this** to prevent
   future code-clarity refactors from breaking it.
4. **Eager evaluation of the unselected regime is fine.** When
   `is_huge = false`, `arg = |x|`, `E = exp(|x|)`. The huge
   formula `(0.5¬∑E)¬∑E = 0.5¬∑exp(2|x|)` is computed eagerly with
   wrong meaning, may overflow to +Inf garbage, but is discarded
   by the cascade. No NaN poisoning because the discarded path
   doesn't feed back. Same pattern as m2bv tanh's regime cascade.
5. **Cancellation analysis matters at the regime boundary, not in
   the middle of a regime.** At |x| = 1.0 (poly‚Üîmedium transition),
   the medium formula loses ~0.21 bits. At |x| = 0.5 (well inside
   poly), the medium formula would lose ~1.6 bits ‚Äî irrelevant
   because we never use it there. So the polynomial regime upper
   bound is dictated by where the medium formula's cancellation
   becomes acceptable, NOT by where the polynomial's accuracy
   degrades. Picking |x| ‚â§ 1.0 puts the boundary at the sweet
   spot.

**Rejected alternatives:**

* **Verbatim Julia stdlib regime split.** Polynomial regime up to
  `|x| ‚â§ 2.1` requires double-double `sinh_kernel` (`two_mul` +
  `exthorner` + `muladd` chain) to keep 1 ULP. Bennett.jl has no
  DD primitives. Adding them for one bead is ~150 LOC scope creep.
  Single-precision Horner on `|x| ‚â§ 1.0` clears the 2-ULP budget
  with comfortable headroom ‚Äî accept the narrower regime.
* **Unified exp form `(E¬≤ - 1/E¬≤)/2`** (Proposer B's initial
  strategy). Empirically 3-4 ULP at `|x| ‚âà 1.4` due to chained
  `soft_fmul` rounding. The mathematically-identical `(E - 1/E)/2`
  for medium fixed it.
* **Verbatim musl `s_sinh.c`.** Uses `expm1` for the small-arg
  branch ‚Äî Bennett.jl has no `soft_expm1`. The Julia-stdlib
  polynomial-in-x¬≤ substitution sidesteps this gap (same pattern
  as m2bv tanh).
