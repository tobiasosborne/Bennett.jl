| T-count                      | 949,088    | —      | —      | —                | —     |

Reproduce: `julia --project benchmark/bc5_sha256_full.jl`.

**Peak live qubits beats PRS15 Bennett projection** (28,133 vs 45,056 upper
bound — we hold strictly fewer qubits live simultaneously). The `n_wires`
and Toffoli numbers reflect the SSA cost of our Bennett construction; the
projection assumes PRS15 numbers scale linearly with 64 rounds, which is
an upper bound — actual PRS15 full-compression would use less due to
in-place reuse. Optimisation paths to close the Toffoli gap:
Cuccaro-self-reversing (Bennett-07r), Cuccaro 2n-3 tightening (Bennett-gsxe),
MemorySSA integration (Bennett-cc0 follow-up).

### Headlines

- **Toffoli-depth**: `:qcla_tree` is 6× shallower than `:shift_add` at W=64
  (64 vs 382) and 4× shallower than `:karatsuba` (64 vs 260). Grows as
  O(log² W) vs. O(W) / O(W^log₂3).
- **Peak live qubits**: `:qcla_tree` achieves peak-live=1 because it's marked
  `self_reversing` in Bennett's construction (P1 — `lr.self_reversing=true`
  skips the outer wrap). `:karatsuba` spikes to 3994 at W=64 due to deep
  recursion.
- **Gate-count trade-off**: `:qcla_tree` uses ~5× more Toffolis than
  `:shift_add` — the price paid for logarithmic depth.

### When to pick what

| Constraint                    | Best strategy  |
|-------------------------------|----------------|
| Classical reversible hardware | `:shift_add`   |
| Ancilla-constrained NISQ      | `:shift_add`   |
| Depth-constrained FTQC        | `:qcla_tree`   |
| Gate-count-optimal at W≥32    | `:karatsuba`   |
| Small W (≤8), simplicity      | `:shift_add`   |
